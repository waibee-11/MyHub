  • The Risk-Return Relationship
    • The less certain the investor is about the projected cash flows, the riskier the investment becomes.
    • Often, uncertainty rises from factors that are difficult to predict and will infuence the firm’s future.
    • Once investors establish the riskiness of the asset, the market allows the price of the asset to adjust so the expected return on the asset fairly compensates the perceived risk.

    • Portfolio of Assets
      • A portfolio of assets is a collection or group of assets held as an investment
      • A portfolio may contain a group of stocks, group of bonds, or even a combination of both
      • A portfolio may also include real estate, gold, options and other income-producing assets

  • Evaluating the Risk for a Porfolio of Assets
    • We cannot simply take a weighted average of all assets in the portfolio, because mutiple combinations of weights can produce a single average value
    • So, we use a measurement called CORRELATION
      • Correlation means that there is a relation between 2 numbers
      • A perfectly positive [1] correlation means that if one number increases, the other number increases by the same value [DIRECT PROPORTION]
      • A perfectly negative correlation [-1] means that if one number increases, the other number decreases by the same value [INVERSE PROPORTION]
    • Diversification
      • Diversification is the art of choosing risky assets that are negatively correlated with each other, which makes the overall portfolio less risky.
