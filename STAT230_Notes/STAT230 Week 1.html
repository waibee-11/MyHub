3 possible definitions of Probability

  1. Classical Method
    • S is called SAMPLE SPACE. It is the set of all possible distinct outcomes of a random experiment.
  1. Relative Frequency Method
    1. The probability of an event in an experiment is the (limiting) proportion or fraction of times the event occurs in a very long (theoretically infinite) series of (independent) repetitions of the experiment.
    1. This method is applicable only in cases where the random experiment is repeatable.
  1. Subjective Probability
    1. The probability of an event is a¬†'best guess'¬†by a person making the statement of the chances that the event will happen. (e.g., a 30% chance of rain)
    1. Whenever you the words ‚Äòexperts predict/suggest‚Äô, it is always subjective probability.

Random Experiments

  • A random experiment satisfies the following 3 criteria
    1. It should have more than one possible outcome
    1. We should be able to repeat the experiment under similar conditions [every repeatition is called a trial]
    1. All the outcomes must be equally likely

Sample Space

  • Here is some more information about sample spaces
    • A sample space¬†S¬†is the set of distinct outcomes for an¬†experiment or process, with the property that in a single trial, one and only¬†one of these outcomes occurs.
    • The outcomes in a sample space are called sample points or simply points

Discrete Sample Spaces

  • Information about discrete sample spaces
    • A¬†discrete sample space S is one with a¬†finite number of sample points or¬†countably many sample points
    • A set S is countable if the elements in the set can be put in a 1-1 correspondence with the positive integers
      • for example, the set of all integers is countable
      • however, the set of all real numbers between 0 and 1 is not countable

Types of Events

  • Events are subsets of the Sample Space S. There are 2 types of events
    1. Simple Events contain just 1 point from the sample space. These are the most basic undividable events
    1. Compound/Complex Events contain more than 1 point from the sample space. A compound event is said to occur if any one of it‚Äôs points occurs

Properties of Complex Events

  • All complex events satisfy the following properties
    • The probability of the occurance of a complex event is the sum of the probability of the individual simple events.
    • The probability of a complex event is always between and including 0 and 1
